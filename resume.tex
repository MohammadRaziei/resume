As a Data Scientist, Polyglot Programmer, and Signal Processing specialist, I operate at the intersection of Telecommunications, Image and Audio Processing, Graph Signal Processing (GSP), and High-Performance Computing (HPC). I hold dual Bachelor’s degrees in Electrical Engineering (Communications) and Computer Engineering (Software) from Amirkabir University of Technology, and a Master’s degree in Biomedical Engineering from Sharif University of Technology, where I focused on MRI image reconstruction.

My primary research focuses on Reconfigurable Intelligent Surface (RIS) Signal Processing, including Beamforming, DoA Estimation, and Localization within complex, multi-element network scenarios. I specialize in utilizing Array Signal Processing (ASP) techniques, such as multi-beamforming and null steering, for high-precision Passive Radio Frequency Localization.

Beyond my research, I am a proficient backend developer with a deep interest in implementing complex algorithms and developing principled, open-source libraries on GitHub. I am also dedicated to teaching and technical writing, having published several specialized courses. I thrive on continuous learning and solving technical challenges within dynamic teams.
